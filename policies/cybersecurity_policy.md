# Cybersecurity & Information Security Policy (Including Reg S-P WISP)

Adopted pursuant to **Regulation S-P (Safeguards Rule)** as amended (compliance required by December 3, 2025 for larger entities), SEC Cybersecurity Risk Management expectations, and fiduciary duty.

## Written Information Security Program (WISP) – Full Reg S-P Implementation
The firm maintains a **Written Information Security Program (WISP)** reasonably designed to:
- Ensure the security and confidentiality of customer information.
- Protect against anticipated threats or hazards to the security or integrity of customer information.
- Protect against unauthorized access to or use of customer information that could result in substantial harm or inconvenience to any customer.

**Customer Information** includes any record containing nonpublic personal information about a customer (or customer of another financial institution) in the firm’s possession or handled on its behalf, in any format.

### Core WISP Elements (Administrative, Technical, Physical Safeguards)
- **Governance**: Designated Qualified Individual (CCO or delegate) oversees the WISP with direct reporting to senior management.
- **Risk Assessment**: Annual (or more frequent) identification of internal/external risks to customer information, including AI-related threats and polymorphic malware.
- **Safeguards**: MFA (or equivalent), encryption, access controls (least privilege, quarterly reviews), data loss prevention tools, endpoint protection.
- **Training**: Annual Reg S-P / cybersecurity training for all supervised persons (included in Training Center with quiz and DocuSign attestation).
- **Testing & Monitoring**: Annual penetration testing + vulnerability scans; quarterly table-top incident response exercises; continuous monitoring.

### Incident Response Program (Mandatory under Amended Reg S-P)
Written policies and procedures reasonably designed to:
- **Detect** unauthorized access to or use of customer information.
- **Respond** and contain incidents (assess nature/scope, contain threats, eradicate root cause).
- **Recover** operations and test restored controls.
- **Assess** whether sensitive customer information was (or is reasonably likely to have been) accessed/used without authorization.

**Customer Notification**:
- Provide clear and conspicuous written notice **as soon as practicable, but no later than 30 days** after becoming aware that sensitive customer information was (or is reasonably likely to have been) accessed/used without authorization.
- Exception: No notice required if, after reasonable investigation, the firm determines the information has not been and is not reasonably likely to be used in a manner causing substantial harm or inconvenience.
- Notification method: Designed to ensure the individual can reasonably be expected to receive it (e.g., email + mail where appropriate).

**Service Provider Oversight**:
- Written policies requiring due diligence and ongoing monitoring of service providers handling customer information.
- Contracts must include: (1) requirement to protect customer information, and (2) notification to the firm **within 72 hours** of any unauthorized access/use of customer information maintained by the provider.
- Firm remains responsible for ensuring timely customer notification even if the breach occurs at a non-covered service provider.

**Recordkeeping** (5 years):
- Documentation of WISP adoption, risk assessments, testing results, incidents, investigations, notification decisions, service provider agreements and oversight, and disposal procedures.

**Annual Review & Reporting**: CCO documents WISP effectiveness and presents findings to senior management with DocuSign sign-off. Adjust program based on business changes, test results, or regulatory updates.

**Disposal Rule**: Proper disposal of customer and consumer information (shredding, erasing, or destroying).

**2026 SEC Exam Focus**: Examiners will review governance, data loss prevention, access controls, account management, incident response/recovery (including ransomware), training on AI/polymorphic malware risks, and operationalization of threat intelligence.
